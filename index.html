Introduction to Fonts

Serif

A serif is a small line attached to the end of a stroke in a letter or symbol

Sans Serif

A typeface without serifs is called sans serif, from the French "sans", meaning "without"

Monospace

All characters occupy the same amount of horizontal space as in a typewriter

Script

Script typefaces typically mimic calligraphy. They are commonly used in logos and can be effectively applied in headers as well.

Display

A typeface that is intended for use at large sizes for headings, rather than for extended passages of body text
